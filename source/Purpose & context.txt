Key learnings & principles

Dual embeddedness is Li's primary differentiator: Her value lies in being genuinely embedded in both Chinese and international academic ecosystems — this should be the core of any positioning, not a secondary note
China-international bridging should be framed strategically, not personally: When raising it in professional contexts (e.g., CSTR meeting), frame Europe-based work as strategically beneficial to the organization's international goals, not as a personal preference
First meetings require listening-heavy calibration: Prep documents should prioritize what to learn over what to convey (~60/40), with prescriptive ideas held in reserve with explicit triggers for when to raise them
Targeted applications over bulk: Li prefers a focused, high-fit application strategy rather than broad volume
Mission alignment is a hard filter: Commercially focused roles (e.g., Elsevier, Autodesk) are likely misaligned even when skills match
Incomplete PhD framing: Should be presented as "Doctoral Studies" with a note that coursework and research were completed, based at CERN — not as terminated
Formal documents: preserve professional tone: Li prefers structured, professional register for formal application materials. Speculative tonal rewrites (e.g., toward "more human") should not be made without specific direction — when tried, she reverted to the original.
CSTR DataCite 4.6 is the primary opening card for any China-facing academic infrastructure conversation
