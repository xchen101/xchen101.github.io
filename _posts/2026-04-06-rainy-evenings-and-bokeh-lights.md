There's something about shooting through rain-streaked glass that turns the ordinary into something cinematic. A few frames from last week's commute.

The bus was late, which meant more time to watch the city blur and refocus through the water on the window. Headlights became soft orbs, traffic signals bled into color fields, and for a few minutes the whole street felt like an impressionist painting.

I didn't plan to shoot that evening. The camera was in my bag from earlier, and the light was too good to ignore.
